Welcome to Preview Week of ICML 2020!

Welcome to the thirty-seventh International Conference on Machine Learning, ICML 2020! This week is the preview week for ICML 2020 - as a registered attendee, you have access to all materials for accepted papers, tutorials and invited talks starting this week: this way, you can watch talks on your schedule and use the time during the conference (July 12-18) for interaction with other conference participants.

The schedule for release of materials is:

  • July 6, 2020: all main conference papers, supplements, and talk videos, together with calendar links to the poster sessions for all main conference papers.

  • July 9, 2020: Invited talk videos, and calendar links to Q&A+Panel discussion sessions of invited talks
                         Tutorial videos, and calendar links to Q&A sessions of tutorials

  • July 12, 2020: Workshop videos.

As a preview of the programming that's available at ICML this year, we have:

  • Sunday July 12: EXPO day. The EXPO is an opportunity to see machine learning in practice, with interactive talks, panels, and demos presented by our sponsors; topics include automated machine learning, reinforcement learning, drug discovery and healthcare, privacy, transparency and explainability, probabilistic machine learning, and datasets and software framework.

  • Monday July 13: Tutorials & Awards. There are 10 tutorials, each of which will be presented at two different time slots. The tutorials are all 3hr long, with four breaks for Q/A and live text-based chat with the presenters as the videos are played back.
    There is also the presentation of the 2020 Awards, including the (previously announced)     Test of Time award and talk by the winners (presented twice), as well as the announcement of the Best Paper Awards. Both take place at 23:00 AOE July 12 and again at 09:00 AOE July 13.

  • Tuesday--Thursday July 14--16: Main Conference. Although videos for main conference papers will have been available since July 6, the three days of the main conference are a time for you to attend Q&A sessions with the authors to discuss work and meet new people.
    On each day of the main conference, there will also be     an invited talk and panel discussion, again at two different times (which vary by day).

  • Thursday July 16: ICML Business Meeting & Townhall. Every year there is a business meeting in which ICML organizers and ICML President discuss the state of ICML, and solicit feedback from the community. This is an opportunity to learn what goes on behind the scenes, and also ask questions and have your voice heard. Two times: 00:00-01:00 AOE and 11:00-12:00 AOE.

  • Friday--Saturday July 17--18: Workshops. You can find more details about workshops here, though note that the details of the schedule may change up until July 12.

  • Throughout:

    • Socials & Mentoring. There will soon be an announcement of social events and mentoring sessions that will take place throughout the conference.

    • Affinity Group Workshops and Events. There are several events during the workshop: Women in Machine Learning (July 13), LatinX in AI (July 13), Queer in AI (every day), and New in ML (July 13).

    • Sponsor Halls. You will have the opportunity to visit virtual "booths" hosted by sponsors. This is a great place to find job or internship opportunities, and to interact with researchers who work at organizations that have sponsored ICML.

Technology: All of the conference can be accessed through this virtual website. The website mainly uses two external tools - Zoom and Rocket.Chat - all video sessions will use Zoom and text based interactions will be via Rocket.Chat. You do not need to register for these separately as the icml.cc login will let you use links to both of these tools automatically. Socials and some workshops will also be using Gather.town (which will also use your icml.cc credentials).

Further details on this, and other aspects of the conference, will soon be available at the FAQ page.

Updated Privacy policy and Code of conduct: The privacy policy for ICML 2020 as well as the code of conduct have been updated to allow for the virtual platforms. By accessing the virtual webpage and tools, all attendees agree to the revised privacy policy and code of conduct. The specific changes in the privacy policy is that it now includes a statement (sharing section, third bullet) that we may share limited registration information with partner platforms to facilitate the virtual conference experience. The code of conduct now includes a broadened scope that covers interactions in virtual spaces and online platforms during the course of ICML.

Timezone: To view the schedule and presentation times in your local time zone, please be sure to set the time zone above.

All programming will be posted on demand following the virtual conference within 4-6 weeks.

Aarti Singh, Hal Daumé III, and David Blei

ICML 2020 Program Co-chairs and General Chair
